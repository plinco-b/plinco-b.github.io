Spend one whenever you need off-network relay. The sheep drift through because your whole job is to count them.

🐶 Puppy system — four outcomes

Puppies are the earn & spend currency of Plinco. You earn them passively (welcome basket, strays, milestones) and spend them for off-network TURN relay. The kennel has two buckets: monthly (PUPgrade subscription) and bonus (one-time grants). Server-authoritative — clients cannot raise balances. The puppy wallet chip is visible on the welcome dashboard whenever you hold puppies.

Proposed new animations — brand expansion PROPOSAL
🐺
Wolf prowl — upgrade / paywall screen
Monetization entry point · v0.17
Wolf paces left-to-right at the bottom of the screen behind the "buy puppies" CTA — same arc system as sheep but on a ground plane (Y fixed, no arc). When off local WiFi, the wolf could appear in the background of the parent on-call screen too, giving honest visual context for why the upgrade matters.
🐶
Puppy bounce — paid mode confirmation
Post-purchase · remote access active
After a successful pupgrade purchase, a puppy bounces in from the bottom and plants itself in the corner of the on-call screen — wagging (rotation ±15°, 0.4s cycle). This visually confirms "your puppy is on guard." When the session ends the puppy scampers off. Reuses the withRepeat + withTiming rotation pattern already in the codebase.
🧱
Wall glow — local WiFi indicator SHIPPED as LocalNetworkBadge
Baby monitoring · parent on-call · v0.19
Shipped as the LocalNetworkBadge tappable chip — a grey brick cluster with "Free & safe" label. Shown in both the baby monitoring screen and the parent TraditionalCheckIn view whenever localNetworkMode is true (free tier / local network). Tapping it surfaces an Alert explaining the free local-network path. No pulse animation — the static chip is cleaner.
🐺🐶
Wolf → Puppy transition — upgrade moment
Off-network warning · upgrade CTA
When off local WiFi without a pupgrade, the wolf prowls in. Tapping "Protect with puppies" triggers a fun transition: a puppy bounds in from the right and chases the wolf off screen. Satisfying one-shot animation (not looping) that rewards the purchase decision. The wolf exit could use the existing leftEdge→offscreen pattern from useArcTranslate.
